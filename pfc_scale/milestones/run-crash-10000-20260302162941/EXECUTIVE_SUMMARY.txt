PFC Crash-Recovery Stability Validation

Scope
A forced hard crash was injected into the worker during a 10,000-message Redis Streams run (run_id run-crash-10000-20260302162941). The worker restarted and reclaimed pending consumer-group messages for deterministic completion under the same signed-decision and signed-receipt controls.

What was tested
- Real Redis Streams consumer group operation on 127.0.0.1:6379.
- Mid-run worker crash after terminal handling of 5,000 messages.
- Recovery using pending-entry reclaim (XAUTOCLAIM with XCLAIM fallback).
- Run-scoped replay verification of signed decision and receipt linkage.

What was proven
- No lost work: run-level receipts reached 10,000 unique message IDs.
- No stuck pending work: XPENDING after completion was 0.
- Deterministic audit integrity: replay_check for run_id reported mismatches=0.
- Governance invariants remained enforced during crash and recovery.

Why it matters
This demonstrates that authority-governed execution remains verifiable and recoverable under process failure, with deterministic run-level evidence sufficient for operational audit and incident review.
